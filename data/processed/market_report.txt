**Dominant Trend: Competition and Focus on Profitability**

The current trend in the Indian e-commerce market is the increasing competition among e-commerce companies, coupled with a shift in focus towards profitability rather than just rapid growth.

As per [Source 2], India's consumer businesses are changing their strategy, focusing on profitability rather than just rapid growth. Digital platforms like e-commerce and quick commerce are helping brands reach customers efficiently, making it crucial to be profitable in this competitive market.

The rise of new players like Switch and PMI in the e-bus market, leading traditional manufacturers to lose market share, as mentioned in [Source 3], also highlights the intense competition in the Indian e-commerce market. To remain competitive, companies like Flipkart and Amazon India are doubling down on their full-funnel approach, taking on Google, Meta, and emerging rivals in India, as stated in [Source 1].

This trend is happening because of the increasing fragmentation of media consumption and the rising competition in the market. As per [Source 1], with media consumption fragmenting, companies need to adopt a full-funnel approach to remain competitive.

Risk Signals
------------
**Trend Analysis:**

The key risk in the ecommerce market is the uncertainty around logistics and delivery, which is impacting profitability and growth for several players.

**Why is it happening?**

Ecommerce companies are facing challenges in maintaining rider availability, particularly during peak seasons like summer and the IPL season, which is expected to rise 25% [Source 6]. Additionally, rising fuel prices are affecting the cost of delivery, making it essential for companies to adopt electric vehicles (EVs) for last-mile deliveries [Source 9]. This shift to EVs is not only driven by sustainability goals but also by increasing costs and supply risks.

**Evidence:**

Food delivery and quick commerce platforms like Flipkart are experiencing uncertainty around rider availability, affecting their growth and profitability [Source 6]. On the other hand, ecommerce and logistics companies are accelerating the shift to electric vehicles (EVs) to mitigate costs and supply risks [Source 9].

**Other related factors:**

While not explicitly mentioned, competition and funding are also relevant factors affecting the ecommerce market. Amazon India, in particular, is investing in its commerce-led play and doubling down on its full-funnel approach [Source 2]. Similarly, brands like Meesho are focusing on trust and technology to target small-town India [Source 8].

However, it's essential to note that the dominant trend identified is related to logistics and delivery, which is impacting the ecommerce market's growth and profitability.
